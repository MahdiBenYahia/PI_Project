This painting, titled 'Catiline Swearing the Conspirators,' was created by the French artist Antoine Watteau. It depicts a scene from Roman history, specifically the conspiracy led by Lucius Sergius Catilina (Catiline) to overthrow the Roman Republic in 63 BC. The purpose of this work was likely to portray a dramatic and pivotal moment from classical history, showcasing Watteau's skill in history painting. The painting belongs to the Baroque period and reflects the culture of 18th-century France, where historical subjects were popular. The central figure is Catiline, shown swearing an oath with his fellow conspirators, including figures like Julius Caesar, who is often interpreted as being present in the background, though his involvement is debated. The scene captures the tension and intrigue of this historical event.
