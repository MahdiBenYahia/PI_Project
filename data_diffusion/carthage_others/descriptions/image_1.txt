Titre: The Capture of Carthage

Description: Painted in the early 18th century by the Venetian artist Giovanni Battista Tiepolo, this dramatic scene portrays the decisive moment when Roman forces breach the walls of Carthage during the Third Punic War. Commissioned to decorate a noble Venetian residence, it aimed to glorify ancient military triumphs and celebrate the power of Rome. The work reflects the Baroque era’s fascination with grand historical narratives, as well as Tiepolo’s mastery of dynamic composition. Key figures include Roman soldiers and Carthaginian defenders, representing a pivotal clash in Mediterranean history.
